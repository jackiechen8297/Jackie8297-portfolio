Executive Summary

During our time at the intro to agricultural tech program for Change Food For Good, we took the time to learn basic construction skills over the course of three seperate days. On each day, we were given a different task to carry out. Our construction training was overseen by a construction company LLC called Soulful Synergy where they instructed us on the basics of construction tools with their respective purposes and usages. We were able to gain hands-on experience with the tools and the machines that are commonly used in a construction worksite.

Project Overview

Background: In this agricultural tech program for Change Food For Good, we learned several skills spanning across web development using html, css, and javascript, sustainable hydroponic farm design in the agricultural portion, and the use of power and hand tools in addition to site safety measures for the construction portion of the program.

  • Day 1:
    • Reviewed safety measures, the construction hierarchy of control, and protective equipment such as helmets, body vests, foot protection, and harnesses.
    • Discussed the "Focus Four" (the four most common ways people get injured) and OSHA certifications.
    • Learned about different types of tools in various categories:
      • Square family
      • Saw family
      • Wrench family
      • Hammer family
      • Demolition family, including wrecking bars
      • Practiced sawing wooden planks using a saw.
  • Day 2:
    • Continued learning about tools and applied the knowledge to build benches using multiple wooden planks.
    • Introduced new tools:
      • Aviation Snips: Used for cutting sheet metal and other tough materials.
        • Red - Left: Designed for cutting curves to the left.
        • Yellow - Straight: Designed for cutting straight lines.
        • Green - Right: Designed for cutting curves to the right.
      • Screwdrivers: Used for driving screws into wood, metal, or other materials. Includes flat-head and Phillips-head types for different screw heads.
      • Drywall Rasps: Hand tools with coarse surfaces, used to smooth and shape the edges of drywall.
      • Wire Cutters: Pliers designed to cut through electrical wire, cable ties, or other small wires.
      • Safety Goggles: Essential protective eyewear designed to shield the eyes from flying debris and dust during construction tasks.
  • Day 3:
    • Learned how to use table saws to cut down PVC pipes for NFT channels used for hydroponic farming.
    • Used power drills to make holes in the PVC pipes for plant placement.
    • Applied a heat gun to warp the PVC pipes as needed.

What is the problem?: Construction worksites often present safety hazards, this creates the potential of workers sustaining injuries.

Factors considered: Lack of safety awareness and lack of personal protective equipment(PPE) can lead to safety hazards. The elimination of safety hazards should always be prioritized.

Recommendations/Suggested Actions: In order to ensure safety among the worksite, conducting safety precautions such as making sure everyone is equipped with every PPE that is required can reduce the risk of sustaining injuries. Prompting the workers to be cautious of any sorrounding safety hazards and reminding them to wear all required personal protective equipment at all times will help eliminate the presence of safety hazards.

Project Analysis

Concepts learned from Construction: Core concepts of maintaining a safe worksite environment were learned during the entire duration of the construction days. A strong emphasis on keeping on all personal protective equipment such as ear plugs, helmets, safety goggles, gloves, and high-visibility vests are used to mitigate the risk of injury. Proper handling and usage of the tools were also important learning lessons since misuse can threaten/compromise the safety of the workers in the vincinity. Last but not least, team work was also emphasized by the instructors as teamwork can increase productivity and improve work efficiency on the worksite.

Challenges

Some of the challenges that may be faced include the initial learning curve of using each tool. Operating the power saw for the first time may also be intimidating for those who have never operated one before.

Solution

Listening to the step by step instructions provided by the instructors led each of us to properly maintain worksite safety and ensure the correct usage of the tools. This approach by the Soulful Synergy instructors encouraged concise communication, reduced likelihood of injury, and boosted our certainity/confidence in operating the tools.

Conclusion

All in all, these construction training days provided great insight into maintaining a safer worksite environment along with. We were able to develop a strong foundation in tool usage and being able to troubleshoot the challenges we faced together while carrying out the instructions of building out the NFT channels and the other components. The skills we learned during the duration of instruction developed important skills of strong-communication, teamwork, and other skills that can be transferrable to any other industries of work.

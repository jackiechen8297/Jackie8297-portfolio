Executive Summary

During our time at the intro to agricultural tech program for Change Food For Good, we took the time to learn basic construction skills over the course of three seperate days. On each day, we were given a different task to carry out. Our construction training was overseen by a construction company LLC called Soulful Synergy where they instructed us on the basics of construction tools with their respective purposes and usages.

Project Overview

Background: In this agricultural tech program for Change Food For Good, we learned several skills spanning across web development using html, css, and javascript, sustainable hydroponic farm design in the agricultural portion, and the use of power and hand tools in addition to site safety measures for the construction portion of the program.

  • Day 1:
    • Reviewed safety measures, the construction hierarchy of control, and protective equipment such as helmets, body vests, foot protection, and harnesses.
    • Discussed the "Focus Four" (the four most common ways people get injured) and OSHA certifications.
    • Learned about different types of tools in various categories:
      • Square family
      • Saw family
      • Wrench family
      • Hammer family
      • Demolition family, including wrecking bars
      • Practiced sawing wooden planks using a saw.
  • Day 2:
    • Continued learning about tools and applied the knowledge to build benches using multiple wooden planks.
    • Introduced new tools:
      • Aviation Snips: Used for cutting sheet metal and other tough materials.
        • Red - Left: Designed for cutting curves to the left.
        • Yellow - Straight: Designed for cutting straight lines.
        • Green - Right: Designed for cutting curves to the right.
      • Screwdrivers: Used for driving screws into wood, metal, or other materials. Includes flat-head and Phillips-head types for different screw heads.
      • Drywall Rasps: Hand tools with coarse surfaces, used to smooth and shape the edges of drywall.
      • Wire Cutters: Pliers designed to cut through electrical wire, cable ties, or other small wires.
      • Safety Goggles: Essential protective eyewear designed to shield the eyes from flying debris and dust during construction tasks.
  • Day 3:
    • Learned how to use table saws to cut down PVC pipes for hydroponic farming.
    • Used power drills to make holes in the PVC pipes for plant placement.
    • Applied a heat gun to warp the PVC pipes as needed.

What is the problem?:The high cost of fresh produce in New York City, coupled with the lack of affordability for healthier food options, has led to a food insecurity issue. In addition, the high cost of living has made it difficult for people to access fresh produce options.

Factors considered: The growing conditions for this system should be taken into account the most. Making sure the water pH and EC levels are within range that allows for optimal growth. The growing Growth medium content is also optional since microgreens aren’t dependent on growing mediums for overall growth. Water must also never make contact with the electricity due to safety concerns.

Recommendations/Suggested Actions: For water, it is highly suggested to use filtered water for sanitation purposes and ensure the pH level to be around 5.5-6.5 with the use of the pH meter. As for safety, make sure that the growing bins are always evenly leveled to make sure the water doesn’t spill and make contact with the electrical components. The potential water leakage can damage the LED lights or cause the entire system to malfunction.

Rationale for Recommendations/Suggested Actions: In order to fufill the recommendations, prior knowledge of the hydroponic systems and the smaller components of the systems should be taught and introduced before being able to build and sustain a hydroponic system. A basic understanding of water pH/EC is also required in order to maintain the livelihood of the hydroponic system.

Challenges

At first I went with the wick method system. I wasn’t aware of its downsides and I decided that the downsides outweigh the upsides. The main downside lies in its design that ultimately impacts the total crop output, the wick rope may not achieve even nutrient distribution.

Solution

Despite me being very enthusiastic about starting the wick system, I decided to go in another direction. I ended up choosing the kratky system method due to its low maintenance, cost-effectiveness and high efficiency.
